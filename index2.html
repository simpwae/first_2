Chapri Kababs!!


About::

Chapli Kabab is a Pakistani spicy meat patty made with ground beef,
dried pomegranate seeds, onions, tomatoes and freshly ground spices.
Charred on the outside, juicy from the inside it pairs wonderfully with some vibrant green chutney and naan.
This easy recipe will give you the perfect balance of smoky, spicy and savoury flavours!

Origins::

Chapli Kabab is a popular street food from Peshawar (a city in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan) and Afghanistan.
The name Chapli refers to its flat shape, resembling a sandal, as 'chapal' means sandal in Urdu.
Some also say that Chapli is the derivation of the Pashto word Chaprikh which means 'flat and thin'.

Ingrediants::

  • Meat
  • Cornmeal
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Coriander
  • Green Chilies
  • Seasonings
  • Eggs
  • Oil

Steps::

  1. In a heated pan, toast the ground spices till they are fragrant.
  2. Transfer them to a spice grinder and blend till roughly crushed but they shouldn't be fine like a powder.
  3. Add these ground seasoning to the minced beef in a large mixing bowl, along with the rest of the ingredients, except egg, tomato and onions.
  4. Mix well till the batter is homogenous. Rest for 30 minutes or even overnight to develop the flavours.
  5. Now add in the onion, tomato and egg. Make sure you squeeze/discard any excess water in the onion and tomatoes. Mix well once again.
  6. Grease your hands and make palm-sized flat kababs. Press with your four fingers to spread the patty around and keep the edges uneven.
  7. Shallow fry in hot oil on medium-high heat for 3-4 minutes each side. Remove on a plate as soon as they are charred.
  8. Serve these chapli kabab hot with mint raita and naan!
